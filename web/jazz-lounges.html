Famous Jazz Lounges

Birdland (New York, USA)

Known as the "Jazz Corner of the World," Birdland opened its doors in 1949 and quickly became a hub for jazz legends such as Charlie Parker, Miles Davis, and John Coltrane. Its intimate setting and acoustic perfection make it one of the most cherished venues for jazz lovers worldwide.

Visit their official website: birdlandjazz.com

Smalls Jazz Club (New York, USA)

Nestled in Greenwich Village, Smalls Jazz Club has been a haven for both emerging talents and established musicians since 1994. Known for its nightly jam sessions, this club offers an authentic and cozy jazz experience.

Visit their official website: smallslive.com

Ronnie Scott's (London, UK)

Established in 1959, Ronnie Scott’s is one of the oldest and most iconic jazz clubs in the world. Hosting legends like Nina Simone, Ella Fitzgerald, and contemporary stars, it remains a cornerstone of the London music scene.

Visit their official website: ronniescotts.co.uk

Blue Note (New York, USA)

Situated in Manhattan’s Greenwich Village, Blue Note is a global symbol of jazz culture. With its world-class performances, it has featured names like Dizzy Gillespie, Chick Corea, and Tony Bennett, becoming a must-visit for jazz enthusiasts.

Visit their official website: bluenotejazz.com

The Village Vanguard (New York, USA)

Opened in 1935, The Village Vanguard is one of the oldest jazz clubs in New York. It has witnessed historic performances and recordings by Bill Evans, John Coltrane, and more, cementing its place as a cultural institution.

Visit their official website: villagevanguard.com

The Baked Potato (Los Angeles, USA)

Known for its quirky atmosphere and unforgettable live sessions, The Baked Potato has been a staple of LA’s jazz scene since 1970. Its intimate space and high-energy performances make it unique.

Visit their official website: thebakedpotato.com

The Jazz Café (London, UK)

Located in Camden, The Jazz Café has become one of the UK’s most dynamic live music venues, blending traditional jazz with funk, soul, and other genres. Its vibrant atmosphere attracts diverse audiences.

Visit their official website: thejazzcafelondon.com
